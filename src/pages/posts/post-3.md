# Formula Student Perspective of an Upperclassman

Published on: 2026-04-16

When I first entered Georgia Tech, I knew that I wanted to be involved in some type of competition team. In highschool, I decided not to do robotics because I prioritized non-profit work. I ended up regreting it. I felt like I was missing out on something. 

Thus, I ended up joining the first competition team I saw, and that was Formula Student Electric (aka HyTech Racing). I saw one team, and I locked it in without thinking anything. Looking back now, I'm super grateful to have ended up here. 

As someone who is now considered an "unc" within the team, I write down my personal experience being in a Formula Student Electric team in university.

Definition: Formula Student Electric - an interdisciplinary engineering competition team where students of all majors and grades gather to design a formula-style fully electric vehicle in one year from scratch and compete in design, business, and racing events with other universities (international)

## Knowledge

To be completely blunt, there was a steep learning curve at HyTech when I first arrived. The leads were just so smart and too occupied with making the car faster that they didn't put much effort into the training modules for freshmen. It was not easy convincing myself to come every workshop night. I even stopped coming for a bit my first semester. 

My second semester, I decided to give it my all. I went every workshop night, even going on non-workshop nights. I do have to say, once you overcome the steep learning curve, it gets infinitely eaiser to pick new things up faster.

To give more insight on how things operated in hytech, there are a solid group of people in the team that end up dedicating a huge chunk of their life into the car. They end up coming every day and staying until 3am just working on car. I also ended up joining this group my second semester. 

As a freshman, there weren't a lot for me to do, but as I came every day and made my prescence known, the leads just started assigning me more tasks. Once I became lead, I started learning even more as I had a responsibility now. It taught me how engineering teams operated in a real world setting where we had deadlines to meet and a car to engineer by the end of the year. 

In conclusion, I learned so much from motorsports. This is also coming from a CS Major btw who worked on data acquisition and operations. Mechanical, Electrical, and Computer Engineering majors have way more things to learn here.

## Community

My normal day back when I was a lead was waking up, going to class, going to the workshop, and staying at the workshop until 3am. I spent majority of my time at the workshop and alongside the people in it. HyTech was work but it was also my closest friends at uni. 

I do have to say that for me, it did make finding friends outside of HyTech a little bit harder as I commuted my freshman year. HyTech was my only community. As an upperclassman now, I look back and regret that I didn't try harder to find other communities. 

## Competition

Going to competition, meant meeting other like-minded students from different universities. I ended up having good people to look forward to meeting once a year at competition from universities like University of Washington, UPENN, and even international universities. 

I met a Korean team back in freshman year, and when I went to study abroad in Korea later on, I was able to go to Formula Student Korea as their spectator!

Several companies also come to these competitions to recruit which I'll go over in the next section.

I also drove for HyTech at competition. I ended up setting a new record when I did. It was fun, but that's it. It was like a bucket list thing. I would definitely advise more girls to try for it if you like racing!

Fun fact: The picture above is actually me in a test session laying down on the ground.

## Network & Career

Everyone and their mothers at HyTech ended up going to some prestigious tech company. Whether it was Apple, SpaceX, Tesla, or a VC-backed start up, everyone was going somewhere. This expanded my network, and now I know people at different companies who can vouch for my work. 

Motorsports in general is super good for certain companies as well. A majority of their intern cohort did some type of motorsport or rocketry team back at their university. You can also find them recruiting at big competitions.

## Conclusion

All in all, 10/10 experience. Did I perhaps invest wayyyy too much time into it? Perhaps so. Do I regret it? Not really. 

As Formula Student is evolving in the states, it's time to turn over a new chapter. Driverless next? Maybe so ...
